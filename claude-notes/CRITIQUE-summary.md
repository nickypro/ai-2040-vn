# Faithfulness critiques (Claude + codex), for v1.0.1

Both reviewers agree: mechanisms are faithful; the PROBLEM is calibration of risk.
Plan A reads as a ~100% success when the source rates p(great future) ~= 42%.

## Top fixes (ranked, both critiques concur)
1. **Add Plan A risk / honesty.** No Plan A failure or near-miss exists. Dramatize at
   least one (approved-but-scary safety case, or a covert-project scare), and add an
   epilogue/overlay beat: "one successful execution of Plan A, not its guaranteed
   result; the authors put only ~42% on reaching a great future."
2. **Freeze the capability meter at the ceiling for 2035-2039.** Currently it climbs
   90->98 during the supposed pause, contradicting "capped at top-human-expert."
   Route real growth (agent population, speed, output, robots) to separate meters.
3. **Add a safety-effort metric** to the dashboard (Safety Researchers / safety compute)
   -- the source LEADS with safety-researcher-years (Plan A 1M vs Plan D 200).
   Also adopt the ai-2040 metric set (Employment / Median Income / Safety Researchers /
   Total Slowdown) + the Human-Labor vs Reliable-Agents dot rows.
4. **Restore the companies' adversarial edge.** Reed currently gets a redemption arc;
   source says companies fight Plan A tooth and nail with lobbying/propaganda.
5. **Plan D**: restore the 3-part indictment (loss of control + power concentration /
   AGI-dictator + WW3 risk), not just misalignment.
6. **Plan B**: restore the authors' verdict -- reject both handoff/war, pivot to Plan A;
   note competent-B has real conditional upside.
7. **Fix the "Race/Slowdown ending of AI 2027" labels** on D/C -- they conflate two
   different documents/taxonomies. Say "resembles themes in AI 2027" or drop.
8. **Covert projects** as the design driver (why closed weights + compute-not-algos).
9. **Global benefit-sharing** (intl dividend $1.2k->$10k, poorest non-Americans ~$1M).
10. **Filtered-vs-open transparency** was a contested design call, not the only option.
11. Middle powers actively clamoring for the deal (multipolar pressure), not just Chen.
12. Treat the Citizen's Dividend as a contested second policy, not an automatic output.

## Keep (both praised)
- The 2029 flowchart (faithful box-for-box), title honesty, and the well-adapted hard
  ideas (persuasion tax, deals-with-misaligned-AIs, orphaned-heir control ceiling,
  5-centuries-in-5-years, lie detectors, space lottery). The art resists triumphalism
  even where the plot doesn't.
