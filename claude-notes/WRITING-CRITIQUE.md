# Writing critique (external + self), for the revision pass

Principles (apply across the whole script):
- Cut "trailer narration": clipped 3-beat declarations that manufacture gravity
  ("It's real. It matters. It changes nothing fundamental.", "That's the plan.",
  "That's the problem.", "It doesn't get less strange."). Replace with a specific
  institutional behavior, physical detail, or consequence.
- Kill vague intensifiers ("very fast, very far away", "the quiet number louder",
  "the discourse sweeps back and forth", "I ran the numbers on that word"). Use
  concrete nouns and mechanisms.
- Let voices diverge. Right now everyone speaks in finished, quotable arguments, so
  Reed/Park/Chen/President read as one policy writer in different name tags. Give each
  a distinct register; let some lines fray, trail, or interrupt.
- Keep the spare-but-warm tone; don't over-correct into dry policy prose either.
- Rename Niko -> Lux everywhere (speaker, sprite prefix, dialogue, narration mentions).
- Pacing is now handled at engine level (autoPace inserts sentence click-pauses), so
  focus on quality; still split any beat longer than ~2-3 sentences into separate
  lines so the textbox isn't overloaded.
